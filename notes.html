Basics of the WWW

The World Wide Web (WWW) is a mindbreakingly huge network of computers spanning the globe. Despite its size and complexity, the web runs mostly on variations of just one protocol, HTTP, brought to you by this guy. When your computer visits a website, it is making an HTTP request to a special kind of computer called a server, just like in the picture below.



Languages

The websites that your computer accesses over the web are written in a number of different languages. Three of the main languages are HTML, CSS, and Javascript. The latter two are usually found mostly in the header area of an HTML document. In this course we will be learning HTML and CSS.

It's OK to forget

An old physics professor of mine used to ask students "As a physicist, what are you allowed to forget?" The answer was "Nothing." This is NOT the case in computer programming, where a google search for the relevant documentation can stand in for an eidetic memory.
